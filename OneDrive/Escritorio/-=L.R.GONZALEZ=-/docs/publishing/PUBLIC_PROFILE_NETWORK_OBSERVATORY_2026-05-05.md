  keep `synthetic`, `demo`, `research`, `falsifier` and `human review` explicit.

## Observacionista Inverso

Question: what can an outside observer, competitor, scraper, buyer or hostile
reader infer from the public surface?

Visible to outsiders:

- product names, repo names, open-core architecture, public repo topics, website
  routes, Gumroad product themes, Sponsors tiers and broad method vocabulary;
- that MEDIOEVO combines books, apps, agent safety, simulation labs and a city
  narrative;
- that the open technology includes ActionGate, ObservationEnvelope, residue
  logs, handoffs, curation and synthetic falsifier labs.

Useful to publish:

- interface contracts, schemas, CLI workflows, templates, examples, README
  quality, public-safe demos, negative results and falsification discipline;
- broad theory as an engineering method: observe, register evidence, gate risky
  action, falsify claims, keep boundaries;
- enough architecture for trust and adoption.

Do not publish:

- private runtime prompts, local orchestration plans, account/session state,
  tokens, Gumroad/Stripe configs, browser auth state or internal automation;
- exact proprietary formulas, thresholds, weights, routing heuristics,
  calibration datasets, decision recipes and high-signal private workflows;
- DUAT Geodia private engineering, GEODIA Social Observatory real data, private
  social simulation inputs, unpublished backtests or inference recipes;
- full books, canon vaults, private lore, RPG/TCG assets, game bridge code,
  unreleased commercial app source, premium templates and support playbooks;
- customer data, family/patrimony details, legal/financial/account continuity
  data, succession details or operational security notes.

Defensive posture:

- publish the method and public-safe tool interfaces;
- sell packaged workflows, UI, templates, support and implementation help;
- keep the private advantage in parameters, data, orchestration, proprietary
  UI/wrappers, brand, books, private game IP and human-curated support.
